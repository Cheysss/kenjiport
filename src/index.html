BasketBall
Basketball is a team sport played on a rectangular court with a hoop at each end. The objective is to score points by shooting the ball through the opponent's hoop while preventing them from doing the same. Each team has five players on the court at a time, and they dribble, pass, and shoot the ball to advance it towards the opposing team's hoop. The team with the most points at the end of the game, typically divided into four quarters, wins. Basketball requires athleticism, skill, and strategic thinking, making it a thrilling and dynamic sport to watch and play.
Volleyball
Volleyball is a team sport played on a rectangular court divided by a net. The objective of the game is to score points by grounding the ball on the opponent's side of the court or forcing them to commit a fault. Each team typically consists of six players on the court at a time, positioned in specific zones based on their roles and responsibilities. The game starts with a serve, where one player hits the ball from behind the back boundary line over the net into the opponent's court. The opposing team must then return the ball over the net within three touches (bump, set, spike) or fewer. The ball continues to be volleyed back and forth until one team fails to return it legally, resulting in a point for the other team. Players use a combination of techniques such as serving, passing, setting, spiking, and blocking to control the ball and outmaneuver the opponent. The first team to reach a predetermined number of points (usually 25) and lead by at least two points wins the set. The match is typically played as the best of three or five sets.
Badminton
Badminton is a racket sport where players use rackets to hit a shuttlecock over a net to the opponent's side of the court. The aim is to score points by making the shuttlecock land within the boundaries of the opponent's court while preventing them from returning it effectively. The game can be played as singles (one player on each side) or doubles (two players on each side). Players or teams score points when the opponent fails to return the shuttlecock or commits a fault. Badminton requires quick reflexes, agility, and strategic thinking as players must anticipate their opponent's moves and react accordingly. The shuttlecock can be hit with various strokes such as clears, smashes, drops, and drives, each serving different purposes during gameplay. Matches are typically played in a best-of-three or best-of-five format, with each game won by the first side to reach 21 points (rally scoring system). If the score reaches 20-20, the side that gains a two-point lead wins the game. The side winning two games wins the match.
